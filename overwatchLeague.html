    • UnderStanding Competition

    In my opinion, the Overwatch pro scene is misunderstood by pretty much everyone in the Overwatch community. It's not their fault, though. Blizzard barely tries to corroborate an understanding of its own esport. There's too many brackets and tiers for someone to just look at and be able to understand. I also think that it is important for players to understand the importance of the pro league in realtion to most of the community. For those that take the game seriously, it works as a common goal between players. In many peoples' eyes, the competitve ladder is a bit of a joke, so, people look up to the Overwatch League and other structured competition.

    The Overwatch pro league has been cut into three distinct official sections. These are referred to as "tiers". Tier 1 would include The Overwatch League and the World Cup. Tier 2 would be Contenders and Contenders Trials. Tier 3 is Open Division. Anything below these three tiers is considered Tier 4. This would include open tournaments and promotional tournaments.

    Overwatch League and World Cup

    The Overwatch League is definitely the most known tournament when it comes to Overwatch. Many community legends made their mark there and works as a goal for players attempting to go pro. The World Cup is also quite popular but lacks the competitive engagement that the League has despite beaing a tournament that is marketed as the highest tier of play. Many of the World Cup's shortcomings are due to a lack of organization and short notice to teams.

    Contenders/Trials

    I'll try to explain Contenders as best as I can, but I barely understand it, myself. The way I see it, Contenders works on a "promotion and relegation" system with teams dropping and gaining tiers based on their performance in the tournament. So if you do poorly in a week of Contenders, ending up in on of the bottom 4 or bottom 8 teams, depending on the week, you'll move down to a lower tier of play. In this example, that lower tier of play would be Contenders Trials. Below Contenders Trials is Open Division. Similarly, you move up through the tiers by winning games and ending up in the top 4 or 8 teams, also depending on the week. From what I can tell, teams only gain points by winning games while in Contenders, not in Trials or OD.

    Open Division

    This is the entry point in Blizzard's "path to pro". On the Overwatch League website, it say something along the lines of, "grind through quick play and competitive and begin your path as a pro in the Open Division tournament and face off against the best in the world." It's something along those lines. Much harder than it sounds. Open Division is not for new players. Although, the rank requirement has been removed, most of the teams that play in Open Division are high masters at least. Most teams below that get knocked out first day. I recommend playing in lower tier, player controlled, tournaments. It'll allow you to get a feel for team play without crushing your dreams into the floor. There are some decent ones like Altitude's Mock Overwatch League and Transendence as well as many others. Going pro is something that takes a lot of time and doesn't pay well at all. Inform yourself more about the subject before deciding you're going to be the next Sinatraa.
